01. Purpose

Weak supervision made past projects hard to control. This document defines who watches the work, what they check, and how problems are escalated so that every hall is built to the same benchmark.

02. Supervision Structure

  • A Site Supervisor / Clerk of Works is appointed for every active site and is present whenever critical work is executed. They report to the PM — never employed by or paid through the Contractor.
  • The design engineers (structural, MEP) perform stage inspections at the hold points in Section 04 and confirm in writing.
  • The PM visits site at least monthly and before every gate.

04. Mandatory Hold Points

Work must not proceed past a hold point until inspected and released in writing on the inspection checklist (template GMF-T-04):

#Hold pointReleased by
1Setting-out — dimensions and orientation checked against drawings and survey beaconsSite Supervisor + Architect
2Foundation excavation — depth and bearing stratum vs. soil reportStructural Engineer
3Foundation reinforcement — size, spacing, cover, before any pourStructural Engineer
4Every structural concrete pour (columns, beams, slabs) — formwork, rebar, mixStructural Engineer or delegated Supervisor
5DPC / ground floor level before blockwork proceedsSite Supervisor
6Roof structure before covering — member sizes, connections, anchorageStructural Engineer
7Services first fix before covering up (conduits, plumbing in walls/slabs)MEP Engineer
8Waterproofing / tanking before backfill or screedSite Supervisor
9Practical completion inspection — full snag listPM + Design Lead + QS

05. Materials and Workmanship Benchmarks

All materials comply with the relevant SON (Standards Organisation of Nigeria) standards. Minimum controls:

  • Cement: SON-certified brands only; stored dry off the ground; no set/lumpy cement used.
  • Reinforcement: mill certificates required per delivery; visibly corroded or undersized bars rejected. Spot-check diameters with calipers.
  • Concrete: mix design approved by the Structural Engineer before first pour; cube tests (or engineer-approved equivalent) for structural pours — minimum one set per pour day; curing enforced for at least 7 days.
  • Blocks: from an approved supplier or site-moulded under supervision; crushing test on samples per source; blocks cured before laying.
  • Sand/aggregate: free of organic material and excessive silt (bottle test on each new source).
  • Roofing, doors, windows, finishes: samples approved and retained before bulk purchase; installed work is compared against the retained sample.
  • Electrical: cables and gear SON-certified; all circuits tested and a test certificate issued before energising.
  • Water/plumbing: pressure test before covering; tanks and borehole installations per manufacturer's data filed in handover records.
Workmanship reference: the drawings and specification extracted from the BIM model are the standard. Where a detail is not specified, the National Building Code applies, and the Design Lead decides.

06. Health, Safety and Site Conduct

  • Contractor provides and enforces basic PPE (helmets, boots) for all workers; scaffolding must be sound and inspected weekly.
  • Contractor's all-risk insurance and workmen's compensation must be valid before site possession and remain valid throughout (checked monthly by the PM).
  • Site secured against unauthorised entry; materials store lockable with a signed issue register.
  • Any injury requiring medical attention, structural distress, or statutory stop-order is reported to the PM the same day and is an exception event under GMF-PG-001 §5.

07. Non-Conformance and Escalation

1.
Defective work is recorded in a non-conformance note (what, where, drawing reference, photo).
2.
Contractor proposes remedy; the relevant engineer approves it.
3.
Rework is at the Contractor's cost unless a change order says otherwise.
4.
Repeated non-conformance (3 notes on the same trade) triggers a formal warning by the PM and is reported to the Board.
5.
Cover-ups without inspection release (Section 04) are treated as non-conforming and may be ordered opened up at the Contractor's cost.

08. Practical Completion and Defects

  • Practical completion only when: hold point 9 inspection passed, snag list agreed with completion dates, test certificates delivered, site cleared.
  • During the defects liability period the Site Supervisor logs defects reported by hall users; the Contractor remedies within agreed timescales; retention is released only after the final defects inspection (Gate 5).
