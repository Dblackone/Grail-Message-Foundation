01. Purpose

This guideline defines how every Grail Message Foundation building project in Nigeria is initiated, planned, designed, built and handed over. Its objectives are:

01
Cost-effectiveness — no project starts without a realistic budget, and no money is spent outside approved procedures.
02
Consistent standards — every hall meets the same quality benchmark regardless of location.
03
Proper supervision and control — clear roles, clear reporting, and a documented trail for every decision.
04
Reusability — designs, models and documents from one project accelerate the next.

No project may proceed to a new phase without completing the gate approval of the previous phase (Section 04).

02. Scope

This guideline applies to new Halls of Worship; extensions, renovations and major repairs of existing halls; and ancillary buildings (administration, accommodation, utilities) on hall sites. Minor maintenance below the threshold set by the Project Board is exempt but must still be recorded in the site logbook.

Every project must have the following roles filled in writing before Phase 1 is approved. One person may hold more than one role only where expressly permitted below.

04. Project Phases and Gate Approvals

Every project passes through six phases. Each phase ends with a gate: a documented approval by the Project Board. Skipping a gate is a breach of this guideline.

Phase 0 — Initiation

·
Need identified (new hall, extension, repair). Deliverable: Project Charter (template GMF-T-01) — purpose, location, rough capacity, order-of-magnitude cost, funding source, proposed team.
·
Gate 0: Board approves the Charter and releases feasibility funds only.

Phase 1 — Feasibility & Land

·
Confirm land title/documents (C of O, deed, survey plan) and site suitability (soil investigation, access, utilities, flooding, topographic survey).
·
Preliminary capacity brief: congregation size, growth allowance, ancillary needs. Budget estimate (Class D, ±30%) prepared by the QS.
·
Gate 1: Board approves site, brief and budget envelope.

Phase 2 — Design

·
Concept design → developed design → technical design, each reviewed against the brief and the cost plan. Full BIM model produced per GMF-BIM-001; drawings are extracted from the model, never drawn separately.
·
Statutory approvals obtained: state development permit / building approval, environmental requirements, fire service where applicable.
·
QS updates the cost plan at each design stage; if the estimate exceeds the envelope, the design is re-worked — the budget is not silently increased.
·
Gate 2: Board approves final design, cost plan (Class B, ±10%), and authority approvals in hand.

Phase 3 — Procurement

·
Bill of Quantities produced from the BIM model. Contractor selection by competitive tender per GMF-CM-001 — minimum three bids for works above the tender threshold.
·
Contract signed before any mobilisation payment; the contract defines scope, price, programme, retention, defects liability period and variation procedure.
·
Gate 3: Board approves contract award and full construction budget.

Phase 4 — Construction

·
Works executed under continuous supervision per GMF-QS-001. Monthly: valuation by QS, progress report by PM to the Board, updated cost forecast.
·
All changes go through the change order procedure — no verbal instructions to the Contractor carry cost or scope effect.
·
Gate 4: Practical completion certified — snag list issued, hall usable.

Phase 5 — Handover & Close-out

·
Defects liability period (minimum 6 months) with retention held. As-built BIM model and documents delivered and archived. Final account agreed and closed by the QS.
·
Lessons-learned report written by the PM and filed — this is how the guideline itself improves.
·
Gate 5: Board closes the project and releases final retention.

A project is in exception (Board must be notified within 7 days) if the forecast final cost exceeds the approved budget by more than 5%, the programme slips more than 4 weeks, or a safety incident, structural concern or statutory stop-order occurs.

06. Documentation Rules

1.
Every project gets a project code: GMF-<STATE>-<TOWN>-<NN> (e.g. GMF-EN-ENUGU-01) — used on all files, drawings and correspondence.
2.
All project documents are filed in the standard folder structure defined in GMF-BIM-001 §7.
3.
Decisions of the Board are minuted; instructions to contractors are written (site instruction forms); payments reference certificates.
4.
As-built records are a condition of final payment to the Contractor.

07. Compliance with Nigerian Law and Standards

  • The National Building Code and the building/planning regulations of the state where the site is located (development permit before construction).
  • Professional registration laws: ARCON (architects), COREN (engineers), QSRBN (quantity surveyors) for roles the law reserves.
  • Standards Organisation of Nigeria (SON) standards for materials (cement, reinforcement, blocks, roofing) — material certificates are checked under GMF-QS-001.
  • Labour, safety and insurance requirements, including contractor's all-risk insurance and workmen's compensation before site possession.
